Financial

There are many aspects to the financial services at Brock, but here are a few that are important and a common struggle for students:

Brock has the 'OneApp' application that allows students to fill in one applications and have it applied to all scholarships they are eligabile to be considered for. This is used to fast track scholarships applications by making it so students don't have to find and apply for each scholarship individually

Students can also use outside sources to look for additional scholarships from ScholarshipsCanada.com, ScholarTree, Canada.ca: Scholarships, StudentAwards.com

The most common source for students to apply to is the government student funding of where they are from. For example, if you have lived in Ontario for 1+ years, you would apply for OSAP

For any questions related to funding sources or how to send payments to the school, students can visit the in-person Financial Services located on the 12th floor of Schmon Tower or reach out to Brock Central via central@brocku.ca or in person on the 3rd floor of Schmon Tower

Student Accessibility Services

Students Accessibility Services (SAS) are the go to place for any disability related services; including but not limited to classroom and testing accomodations

In order to register online with SAS at Brock, you need to complete the related paperwork and will then be assigned a team (orange, green, blue, etc.) consisting of two case workers whom you can reach out to.

A case worker is the person who you can bring your concerns to, questions about accomodations or available services, reach out to professors regarding accessability needs, or they can modify your accomodations if anything chanes

If you are unsure of something, you can visit the SAS page at brocku.ca, visit them in person on the 4th floor of Schmon Tower, through a live chat on their website, or via email at asksas@brocku.ca

To locate your personal accomodations for each class and to confirm you accomodations for exams, see the "Navigatin MyBrockU" section below.

Career

When it's time to apply for a summer job or your new career after university, brock has many resources to support your journey.

Careerzone offers free headshots, resume help, interview prep, and workshops for many things which can be found on their website.

For any questions, the first place to check is the brock careerzone webite: careerzone.brocku.ca. The second option, if you don't see what you are looking for, is to email them at career@brocku.ca. Your third option is to visit the careerzone in person. They are located at Market Eatery, next to the stairs.

Prototyping/Crafts

While the main campus may not be known for its fine arts like the Marilyn I. Walker campus, the Makerspace is a great source of artistic opportunity for students

Some have called the Makerspace the most obvious but least noticed place on campus. The few that do notice it seem to be afraid to enter, thinking it is either a class space or only for students from specific majors. This is luckily not the case and the Makerspace is a lovely space with very welcoming staff.

If you are curious, here's some of the possible things to do in the Makerspace

  • Sewing
  • Embroidery
  • 3D design
  • 3D printing
  • Miniature painting
  • Podcasting
  • Audio/video editing
  • Die cutting
  • Cricut stickers or iron-ons
  • Button making
  • Engraving
  • Wood burning (via machine)
  • And so much more!

If any of these sound interesting, but you don't know how to do them, there are workshops you can sign up for or ask a staff member and they'll happily teach you a new skill for your repertiore.

Academic help/tutoring

Brock offers a discounted price of $25 per hour on tutoring services for students versus the pricing for community members of $40 per hour. Tutering is offered in person or online. Payment is made online at the Brocku.ca tutoring services page.

If you are looking for tutoring in science related courses, you may be able to receive help from the science drop in centre on floor 1 next to Market Eatery. This service is offered free of charge while brock pays tutors to be present for certain times. The schedule changes each term, but it can be located by a simple google search.

Transportation

Walk

Walking to school is very ecofriendly, healthy, and free! The other side of the matter is the weather that comes with living in Canada. While walking may not be a feasible option all year, it is a great option when possible. A valuable suggestion is to check the weather when you wake up in the morning for the whole day so that you also know how you should be getting home

Bike

Biking also is a very ecofriendly, healthy, and free option, but it is still important to choose your method of getting to school based on the weather. It is also still a valuable suggestion to check the weather when you wake up in the morning for the whole day so that you also know how you should be getting home

Bus

Taking the bus may not provide the health benefits you could get from the exercise of walking or biking, but it is very ecofriendly, usually faster than walking or biking, and can be useful if you live farther from the Brock campus.

One perk of Brock University is that you get a discounted bus pass built into your student id card each year. This is meant to help each student have the same access to off campus resources and it is a positive & unique part of going to Brock.

Drive

While driving is not ecofriendly or provide a means of exercise, it is what many Brock students opt for. It is a means to independence and freedom from the scedules outsied factors (i.e., bus times).

This is by far the most expensive option - here are some pricing factors to consider:

  • Car purchase price
  • Gas price
  • Car maintenance price
  • Car insurance price
  • Parking pass price

The parking passes at brock vary in price depending on location. The options consist primarily of lots 1-3 with extra options (i.e., lot b) depending on if you are in specific residences. Lot 1 is the most expense as it is closest to the school and it decreases from there for 3 and then 2.

By accessing the Brocku.ca parking-services page, you can select the permit location you want, add your liscence plate, and you're set!

In order to avoid accidental charges on your account the following are things to remember:

  • If you switch cars for a day, make sure you've added the new liscence plate information into your account
  • If you upgraded to another lot, make sure you have paid and recieved confirmation of the change to avoid a ticket for being in the wrong lot
  • If you are in lot 3, use the signage to know which side of the lot is for village residence members and which is for general permit holders

If you do not want to buy a full term or year permit, but only need occasional parking, Brock utilizes an app called HONK for which you can scan a QR code on the signs in each lot or put the zone code into the app. Once in the app, add your amount of parking time, liscence plate information, and card information for payment.

If you happen to be givin a ticket for something that was not your own mistake, you can contest it through the same Brocku.ca site under the 'appeal' tab.
